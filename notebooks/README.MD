# Customer Segmentation using RFM Analysis

## Project Overview

Customer segmentation is a data-driven approach used to categorize customers based on their purchasing behavior. By understanding customer value and engagement patterns, businesses can design targeted marketing strategies, improve customer retention, and maximize revenue.

This project performs Customer Segmentation using the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework on retail transaction data. The objective is to identify different customer groups such as High-Value Customers, Loyal Customers, New Customers, and At-Risk Customers.

---

## Business Objective

The goal of this analysis is to:

* Understand customer purchasing behavior.
* Identify valuable and loyal customers.
* Detect customers who may be at risk of churn.
* Support personalized marketing and retention strategies.
* Generate actionable business insights from transactional data.

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains retail transaction records with the following attributes:

* Invoice Number
* Product Information
* Quantity Purchased
* Transaction Date
* Unit Price
* Customer ID
* Country

The data is cleaned and processed before performing customer segmentation.

---

## Methodology

The project follows the following analytical workflow:

1. Data Loading
2. Data Cleaning
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
4. Feature Engineering
5. RFM Analysis
6. Customer Segmentation
7. Data Visualization
8. Business Recommendations

---

## RFM Analysis

RFM Analysis evaluates customer behavior using three metrics:

### Recency (R)

Measures how recently a customer made a purchase.

### Frequency (F)

Measures how often a customer makes purchases.

### Monetary (M)

Measures the total amount spent by a customer.

These metrics help identify customer value and support strategic decision-making.

## Expected Outcome

The analysis will segment customers into meaningful groups and provide business recommendations for customer acquisition, retention, and revenue growth.
